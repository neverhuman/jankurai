Language history is compression history.

Use this when choosing a language:
- ask what cost it compresses
- ask what cost it pushes downstream
- prefer compile-time rejection for dangerous mistakes
- treat syntax as a trade package, not a virtue signal

Good languages compress repeated human decisions.
Bad languages only move the work into debugging, review, or production.

Rule of thumb: choose the language that makes the worst likely mistake easiest to reject, localize, and repair.
