This needs to become the central spine of the paper. Right now, the paper says this in pieces. It needs to say it as the main revolution.

# The strongest final framing

The paper should not be framed as:

> an AI merge standard

That is too narrow.

It should be framed as:

> **a continuous repository alignment standard for the AI era**

The difference matters. “Merge” is the enforcement point. “Continuous alignment” is the bigger promise.

The final message should be:

> AI makes code generation cheap. Jankurai makes repository alignment auditable. It detects vibe-coding artifacts, routes them to proof, produces receipts, binds merge decisions into witnesses, and turns missing evidence into repair queues. That is the new standard: not faster coding, but continuously verifiable software evolution.

And the public hook should be:

> **From vibe coding to verifiable merge.**
